Effective Bed Bug Elimination

Bed bugs are notorious for their resilience and rapid reproduction. DIY methods often fail, leading to spreading infestations. Our professional treatment combines multiple strategies to ensure complete elimination in a single treatment when possible.

Warning: Bed bugs multiply quickly. A single female can lay 200-250 eggs in her lifetime. Early professional treatment is essential.

Heat Treatment

Whole-room heat treatment raises temperatures to lethal levels for bed bugs in all life stages, including eggs.

Chemical Treatment

Targeted application of EPA-registered insecticides in cracks, crevices, and harborages for residual protection.

K9 Detection

Specially trained bed bug detection dogs locate infestations with up to 97% accuracy, even in early stages.

Follow-Up Inspections

We verify elimination success with follow-up inspections and monitoring devices.

Signs of Bed Bugs

Bite Marks

Red, itchy welts often in lines or clusters on exposed skin

Blood Stains

Small rust-colored spots on sheets or mattresses

Dark Spots

Bed bug excrement appears as tiny black dots

Live Bugs

Apple seed-sized insects in mattress seams, box springs, or furniture

Treatment Methods

Our most effective and popular treatment method. We raise the temperature of the affected area to 120-140°F, which is lethal to bed bugs in all life stages.

Advantages:
  • Kills bed bugs and eggs in a single treatment
  • No chemical residue
  • Penetrates walls, furniture, and hard-to-reach areas
  • Environmentally friendly
  • Usually completed in 6-8 hours

Strategic application of insecticides in harborages and travel paths. Often combined with heat for maximum effectiveness.

Includes:
  • Crack and crevice treatment
  • Residual dusts in wall voids
  • Mattress encasements
  • Interceptors for bed legs
  • Follow-up applications as needed

High-temperature steam is applied directly to mattresses, box springs, furniture, and baseboards. Ideal for sensitive items that cannot be heat-treated.

  • Instant kill on contact
  • Safe for most fabrics
  • No chemicals used
  • Perfect for localized treatments

For severe infestations, we combine multiple methods for maximum effectiveness:

  • Heat treatment for main living areas
  • Chemical barriers for prevention
  • Steam for delicate items
  • Physical removal with HEPA vacuum
  • Encasements for mattresses
  • Follow-up monitoring

Preparing for Treatment

Proper preparation is essential for treatment success. We provide detailed instructions, but key steps include:

  • Wash and dry bedding on high heat
  • Remove clutter from floors
  • Pull furniture away from walls
  • Unplug electronics
  • Remove heat-sensitive items
  • Vacuum thoroughly before treatment
